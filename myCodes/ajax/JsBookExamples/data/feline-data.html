Cougar

The cougar, also known as the puma, mountain lion, panther, or catamount, is a large cat native to the Americas. It is the most widespread of any large wild terrestrial mammal in the Western Hemisphere. Cougars are muscular and have large paws with retractable claws. They are capable of climbing and often retreat to higher ground. They are the world's fourth largest wildcat after lions, tigers, and jaguars.

Lion

The lion, a large feline native to both Africa and India, is easily recognized by its muscular build, short, rounded head, and the dark, hairy tuft at the end of its tail. Lions exhibit sexual dimorphism, with males and females displaying distinct physical traits. Adult male lions are particularly notable for their manes, the thick, flowing hair that adorns their necks and heads. As carnivores, lions live in social groups known as prides. Sadly, they are vulnerable to extinction due to habitat loss and poaching.

Black Panther

Black panthers are large felines that belong to the big cat family. They are known for their black fur, which is actually a result of a melanistic color variation. This means that they have an excess of melanin, the pigment that gives animals their color. The most common species of black panther are the jaguar (Panthera onca) and the leopard (Panthera pardus). Black panthers are powerful and agile animals with strong bodies, long tails for balance, and sharp claws and teeth. They are solitary hunters and are found in various habitats across the world, including forests, grasslands, and mountains.
